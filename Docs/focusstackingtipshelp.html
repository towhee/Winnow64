Focus Stacking Tips

Focus stacking combines a series of images shot at different focus distances into a single image with greater depth of field. Winnow can process one stack at a time or a batch of multiple stacks in one operation.

Source Images

Winnow can receive the source images for a stack in two ways:

  • Select the source images directly in Winnow and stack them. This is the normal workflow when you are browsing a folder in Winnow and choose the images yourself.
  • Select the source images in another program, such as Lightroom, and export them to the FocusStack winnet. This launches Winnow, where the images are processed and the final fused image is created.

Finding the winnets

The winnets are small export droplets that Winnow keeps in its per-user data folder. To set up an export from Lightroom (or another program) you point it at the FocusStack winnet in that folder:

  • On macOS, open Finder, choose Go > Go to Folder… and enter ~/Library/Application Support/Winnow/Winnets.
  • On Windows, open Explorer and enter %APPDATA%\Winnow\Winnets in the address bar (this expands to C:\Users\<you>\AppData\Roaming\Winnow\Winnets).

Batch selection of multiple stacks

You do not have to stack one group of images at a time. Select the images for several stacks and Winnow will detect the separate stacks and process them in sequence as a batch.

  • Select all of the images that belong to the stacks you want to process.
  • Winnow groups the selection into individual stacks and queues each one.
  • Any miscellaneous images in the selection that are not part of a stack are detected and ignored.
  • Each stack is processed in turn and its result is produced before the next stack begins.

Batch processing lets you set up many stacks and leave Winnow to work through them unattended.

Status progress bar

While a stack or batch is being processed, progress is updated in the status bar. You can continue to use Winnow while the focus stacking is being processed.
